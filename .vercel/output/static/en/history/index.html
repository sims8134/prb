History

"Those who do not learn from history are condemned to repeat it."

This website has been created for cultural and historical purposes only. It is in no way the tool of any political ideology.
9 Sept. 1944

The Coup

Communist partisans backed by the USSR overthrow the royalist government allied with Nazi Germany. The beginning of socialism in Bulgaria.

15 Sept. 1946

Referendum — People's Republic

The 1946 referendum takes place on September 15th. The country is declared a People's Republic.

6 Dec. 1947

Georgi Dimitrov Constitution

The Georgi Dimitrov Constitution is adopted and remains in force until 1971.

2 Jul. 1949

Death of Georgi Dimitrov

Georgi Dimitrov dies on July 2nd, 1949, and is displayed in the Georgi Dimitrov Mausoleum.

20 Dec. 1949

Valko Chervenkov takes power

Valko Chervenkov succeeds Georgi Dimitrov as General Secretary of the Communist Party until 1954.

4 Mar. 1954

Todor Zhivkov rises to power

Todor Zhivkov succeeds Valko Chervenkov as head of the Communist Party. He becomes the longest-serving leader in modern Bulgarian history.

28 Mar. 1965

The April Conspiracy

The "April Conspiracy" is revealed on March 28th, 1965. The attempted coup aimed to overthrow Todor Zhivkov.

8 May 1971

Zhivkov Constitution

The "Zhivkov Constitution" is established and remains in force until the end of the People's Republic of Bulgaria.

10 Apr. 1979

First Bulgarian Cosmonaut

Bulgaria's first cosmonaut Georgi Ivanov is launched into space on the Soyuz 33 mission.

6 May 1981

1300 Years of Bulgaria

The Bulgarian nation celebrates 1300 years of existence. The largest military parade in Sofia's history takes place on this occasion.

23 Aug. 1981

Buzludzha Monument Inaugurated

The monumental house of the Bulgarian Communist Party "Buzludzha" is inaugurated.

10 Nov. 1989

End of Zhivkov's Rule

One day after the fall of the Berlin Wall, communist leader Todor Zhivkov is removed from his position as General Secretary of the Communist Party.

15 Nov. 1990

End of the People's Republic

The Assembly votes to rename the country the Republic of Bulgaria and removes the communist emblem from the national flag.
